tarot study

There's a huge amount of literature about the tarot, from its history to its use for divination to its use for ritual magic. All of these can be interesting and valuable depending upon where you are on your journey. I've put together a short list of the books that I have found useful here.

But the first thing is to get your own tarot deck if you don't have one already. There are many available and choice is really a personal one - what works for you? My journey started with Waite-Smith, then Paul Case's B.O.T.A. version, then Crowley and Harris's epic deck. These days I've gone back to basics with the Marseille tarot - specifically the late Jean-Claude Flornoy's restoration of the tarot of Jean Dodal - the card images used on this website. You can get this deck from the Tarot History website.

I suggest that you start by simply looking at each card. Take your time. Lay the cards out in rows and columns, by suits and by numbers, and look for relationships. Allow each image to become a part of you, to live in your consciousness.
