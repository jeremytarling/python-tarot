Welcome to the Tarot!

Having trouble making a decision? Worried about the future? Concerned that secret forces are controlling your destiny? Then it's time to consult the Tarot..

Think about your question and chose one or three cards:

draw one random card
a word of advice in answer to your question

draw three random cards
advice with more context - for example the past, present and future of the matter - or perhaps you, what opposes you, and how to overcome it

tarot study
the tarot is much more than a divinatory tool...

WTF?

This little website is a simple application written in Python to draw one or three randomly selected tarot cards from a dictionary of 78 descriptions and image references. The source code is here - there are no license restrictions, please feel free to fork or re-use.

The tarot images and descriptions are the late Dr Yoav Ben Dov's excellent restoration of Nicholas Conver's deck from 1760, which he kindly made available under the Creative Commons by-nc-sa licence - please be sure to link back to his website if you use them.

As for me, my name is Jeremy Tarling and I work as a metadata specialist at the BBC. I've been fascinated by the tarot since childhood when my aunty first introduced me to the cards.
