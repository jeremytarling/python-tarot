tarot study

I've spent many years studying the tarot, and while I love the complexity of the decks inspired by Antoinne Court and his successors I humbly suggest that you don't need to be an expert in qabalah, astrology or alchemy to learn the tarot. One might even speculate that the cards were designed the way they are so that you don't need to know all that stuff ðŸ™‚.

So, the first thing is to get your own tarot deck. The choice of deck is really a personal one - what works for you? My journey started with Waite-Smith, then Paul Case's B.O.T.A. version, then of course Crowley and Harris's epic deck. These days I've gone back to basics with Marseille tarot - specifically the late Jean-Claude Flornoy's lovely restorations of the tarots of Jean Noblet and Jean Dodal - you can get them from his website Tarot History.

I suggest that you start by simply looking at each card. Take your time. Learn each image so well that you can draw it in detail on a blank piece of paper. Consider the posture and gestures of the figures in the trumps and court cards, and the arrangement and decorations of the symbols in the cards of the four suits. Lay the cards out in rows and columns, by suits and by numbers, and look for relationships. Let these images tell you their story. Allow each image to become a part of you, to live in your consciousness.

Happy travels!
